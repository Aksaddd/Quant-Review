Fractional Exponents

Now we've covered all possible integer exponents, but what about fractional exponents? For example, what is 251/2? We know that 52 = 25, and if we take the leap of faith that all our previous rules hold for fractional powers as well, we can raise each side to the 1/2 power: (52)1' 2 = 251/2. Since (52)^ 2 - 52^ 2) = 5 1 = 5, we then have 25^2 = 5. The exponent 1/2 has a special name, the square root, and it also has a special symbol associated with it, V~. (This symbol is called a radical.) Thus we write 251/2 = V25 = 5. When asked for the square root of a number, say 81, we find the number which, when squared, equals 81. Since we get 81 when we square 9, V81 = 9. EXAMPLE 1-6 Which two integers is V55 between? Solution: Since V49 = 7 is smaller than V55 and V64 = 8 is larger, we know that V55 is between 7 and 8 .
