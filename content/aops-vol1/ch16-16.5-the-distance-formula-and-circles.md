The Distance Formula and Circles

Given the coordinates (x\, y\) and {x2, 1/2) of two points in the plane, how far apart are they? The answer is a simple application of the Pythagorean Theorem. To get from the first to the second, we go right a distance (x2 - *i) and up a distance (y2 - yi). These two distances form the legs of a right triangle as shown. The hypotenuse of that triangle is the distance we want, which we'll call d. Thus, by the Pythagorean Theorem, we have d2 = (x2 - xi)2 + (y2 - yi)2, so that d = \J(x2 -x\ )2 + (y2 - yi)2. This formula is called the distance formula.
