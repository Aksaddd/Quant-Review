## Role Overview

The person who bridges learning science and product. They are the internal expert on the six-book synthesis and translate the evidence base into feature specifications, validate that implementations actually reflect the science, and own all curriculum architecture.

**Non-negotiable background:** cognitive science or educational psychology with applied experience in curriculum design.

## Core Responsibilities

- Own the full learning science specification for every feature — SM-2 calibration, interleaving schedules, desirable difficulty parameters
- Design the **prerequisite gating map** across all chapters (e.g., Chapter 4 → 70% mastery before Chapter 5 unlocks)
- Define **mistake taxonomy** categories: conceptual error, calculation error, formula recall failure, misread problem
- Specify the **memory palace curriculum** for high-frequency formulas (Black-Scholes, Bayes, Ito's lemma, distributions)
- Author the **Technique Atlas** — classify all 200+ problems by their underlying technique and prerequisite concept
- Write session-length nudge copy and sleep/consolidation UX copy grounded in Dehaene and Oakley's research
- Define the XP and leveling system in collaboration with the Gamification Designer (McGonigal's four intrinsic rewards framework)
- Run bi-weekly learning outcome reviews: analyze SM-2 interval accuracy, error taxonomy distributions, hint usage trends
