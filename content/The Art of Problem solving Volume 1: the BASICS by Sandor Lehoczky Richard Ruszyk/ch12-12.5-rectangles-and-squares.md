Rectangles and Squares

A rectangle is a quadrilateral with four equal angles. Since the sum of the angles in a quadrilateral is 360°, the four angles of a rectangle are right angles. Since lA + IB = IB + /.C = 180°, we find AB ||CD and BC ||AD. Thus all rectangles are parallelograms, so all that is true of parallelograms is also true of rectangles. The two side lengths of rectangles are commonly called the length, I, and the width, w, where the length is usually the longer side and the width the shorter. The perimeter of a rectangle is 21 + 2w and the area is the product of these, [ABCD] = Iw. Furthermore, the diagonals not only bisect each other, but as a result of the Pythagorean Theorem they are equal, with length V/2 + w 2. (Prove it!)
