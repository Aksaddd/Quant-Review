24.5 Sequences We have spent most of the chapter dealing with series. Sequences are even simpler, though: a sequence is just a list of terms. For example, 1,1,1,1,1; or 2,4,6,8; or 1, - 1. Just like a series, a sequence can stop after a while or go on forever. Like the J2 notation for series, there is a shorthand for sequences: write the nth term in curly braces, where n is a dummy variable. Thus the three sequences above can be written {1}, {2n), and {(-1)"+1 £). The dummy variable is assumed to start at 1 and increase by l's.
