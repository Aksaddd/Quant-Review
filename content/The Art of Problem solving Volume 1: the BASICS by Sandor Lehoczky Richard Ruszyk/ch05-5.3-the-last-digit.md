The Last Digit

No matter what number base we are using, often the most important digit of a number is the last digit. Why should the last digit be so important? There is a very simple reason: if we want to know the last digit of the sum or product of two numbers, all we have to do is find the sum or product of their last digits and take the last digit of that result. EXERCISE 5-9 Convince yourself that the previous statement is true. Find the last digits of 34 •17 and 34 + 17, first by actually doing the multiplication and addition, then by taking the last digits and just multiplying or adding those. Explain why this works. The method of the last digit works in any number base and can be used to prove some very useful facts. EXERCISE 5-10 In base 10, what digits can the last digit of the square of an integer not be? EXAMPLE 5-4 Find the units digit of 742 + 427. Solution: To find the last digit of 742 + 427, we find the last digit of each of the two quanti­ ties in the sum. To find the last digit of 742, we break it up into a product of 7's. Since 72 = 49,72 ends in 9. Since 74 = 72 •72, it ends in the same number as 9 •9 ends in, or 1. Now we write 742 = (74)10 •72. Since 74 ends in 1, the last digit of (74)10 is the same as the last digit of the product of ten l's, or 1. Finally, since (74)10 ends in 1 and 72 ends in 9, 742 ends in 1 •9 = 9. In finding the last digit of 427, the tens digit is irrelevant because it does not contribute to the units digit of the product. Hence we are only concerned with 27. Since this is 128, the last digit of 427 is 8 . (Make sure you see why the last digit of 427 is the same as that of 2 7.) Completing our problem, 742 + 427 ends in the same digit as 9 + 8, or 7.
