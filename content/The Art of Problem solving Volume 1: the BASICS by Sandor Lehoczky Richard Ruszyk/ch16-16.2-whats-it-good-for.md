However, there are choices of coordinates which may have a simplifying effect (which is the sole purpose of coordinates to begin with). By far the most important such coordinate system is that of polar coordinates. In this setup, a point is labelled by the ordered pair (r, 0), where r is the distance from the origin and d is the angle with respect to a chosen axis, usually taken to be the positive Cartesian x-axis. The "grid" of polar coordinates is shown in the figure. The circles represent increasing r, the lines increasing 6. Do you see why each set of coordinates (r, 6) identifies one and only one point? There exist other ways to put coordinates on the plane, but polar and Cartesian are plenty for most purposes. Because Cartesian coordi­ nates are the most generally useful, any coordinates in which the coordinate system is not specified may be assumed to be Cartesian.
